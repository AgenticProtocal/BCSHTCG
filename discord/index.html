The Problem

Good design is a hypothesis.
Data is the proof.

?

Is Aiko at 10 Power too dominant?

Does she win every Node she touches? Is 6 Bitzen the only viable late-game play? You designed her to be powerful — but is she too powerful?

?

Does Hivemantle break the game?

Obelith Muskl contributes +3 alone. That's instant Hivemantle 3 — Corruption spreads to adjacent Nodes. Does Nexarch Swarm dominate everything?

?

Are Bonded pairs balanced?

Ethan copies an ability AND Zara gets +2 Power. Nova auto-freezes AND Frost acts next. Do these combos create unbeatable two-card packages?

You cannot answer these from documents. You answer them from hundreds of matches. The Discord bot generates those matches.

Approach

Three phases,
not one

Balance the foundation first. Then expand from confidence. Don't stack unknowns on unknowns.

Phase 1 — Weeks 1-2

Play

Build the bot. Load existing 24+ cards. Open it. Let them play. Generate data.

  • Build bot with AI coding agent
  • All 24 cards with designed stats
  • 4 pre-built deck templates
  • Auto-track all balance metrics
  • First 100+ matches
Phase 2 — Weeks 3-6

Balance

Weekly Balance Reports. Data-driven tuning. Community proposes, team decides.

  • Monday: auto Balance Report
  • Discussion in #balance-lab
  • Friday: team decides adjustments
  • Weekend: updated cards go live
  • Core roster stabilized by Week 6
Phase 3 — Weeks 7+

Expand

Core balanced. Now add new cards. Hero Creation concepts enter Card Lab.

  • Card Lab opens for new designs
  • Hero Creation → Card Lab pipeline
  • Deck building unlocked
  • Weekly tournaments begin
  • Community-designed cards finalized

Why this order: If you let the community design new cards before the existing roster is balanced, you're stacking unknowns on unknowns. Balance the foundation first. Then expand from a position of confidence.
